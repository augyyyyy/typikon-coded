# Encyclopedia of Sidalen Logic (The 4 Points)

> [!IMPORTANT]
> **Source of Truth:** Dolnytsky Typikon, Part II (General Rubrics) and Part III (Menaion).
> This document defines the precise logic for the "Interludes" of Matins: the items lying between the Kathismata and the Canon Odes.

## The Four Cardinal Points
In the Matins structure (Polyeleos Rank and above), there are four critical "slots" for Sessional Hymns (Sidalnyi) and their cognates (Hypakoe, Kontakion).

| Point | Location | Liturgical Function |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **I** | After Kathisma I | First Sessional Set |
| **II** | After Kathisma II | Second Sessional Set |
| **III** | After Polyeleos (Kath. XIX) | The "Third" Slot (Hypakoe / Polyeleos Sessional) |
| **IV** | After Canon Ode III | The "Middle" Interlude (Sidalen / Kontakion Shift) |

---

## Scenario 1: Sunday + Polyeleos Saint
**Context:** A standard Sunday occurring with a Saint of Rank 3 (Polyeleos) or higher (Vigil).
*Example: St. Demetrius (Oct 26) on a Sunday.*

### Point I: After Kathisma I
*   **Logic:** **Sunday Priorities (Octoechos).**
*   **Content:**
    1.  Resurrectional Sidalen 1 (Tone of Week).
    2.  Glory: Resurrectional Sidalen 2.
    3.  Both now: Resurrectional Theotokion.
    *   *The Saint is suppressed here.*

### Point II: After Kathisma II
*   **Logic:** **Sunday Priorities (Octoechos).**
*   **Content:**
    1.  Resurrectional Sidalen 1 (Tone of Week).
    2.  Glory: Resurrectional Sidalen 2.
    3.  Both now: Resurrectional Theotokion.
    *   *The Saint is suppressed here.*

### Point III: After Polyeleos (Kathisma XIX)
*   **Logic:** **The "Double Stack".** Sunday Hypakoe takes precedence, but the Saint's Sessionals must be recovered.
*   **Content:**
    1.  **Hypakoe** of the Resurrection (Tone of Week).
    2.  Sessional of the Saint (1st).
    3.  Sessional of the Saint (2nd) [if exists].
    4.  **Glory:** Polyeleos Sessional of the Saint.
    5.  **Both now:** Theotokion of the Polyeleos Sessional.
    *   *Citation: Dolnytsky Pt II, Line 157.*

### Point IV: After Canon Ode III
*   **Logic:** **The Kontakion Shift.**
*   **Content:**
    1.  **Kontakion** of the Saint (Moved from Ode VI).
    2.  **Ikos** of the Saint (Moved from Ode VI).
    3.  **Sessional** of the Saint (Specific for Ode III).
    4.  **Glory, Both now:** Theotokion (of the Saint's Sessional).
    *   *Note:* The Saint's Kontakion is moved here because the Resurrectional Kontakion takes the "High Place" at Ode VI.

### Point V: After Canon Ode VI
*   **Logic:** **Sunday Supremacy.**
*   **Content:**
    1.  **Kontakion** of the Resurrection.
    2.  **Ikos** of the Resurrection.

## Scenario 2: Sunday + Great Feast (Lord or Theotokos)
**Context:** A Great Feast falls on a Sunday.
*Example: Exaltation of the Cross (Sept 14) on Sunday.*

### Point I: After Kathisma I
*   **Logic:** **Feast Priority.** (Overrides Octoechos).
*   **Content:**
    1.  Sessional of the Feast.
    2.  Glory, Both now: Sessional of the Feast.
    *   *Octoechos Resurrectional Sessionals are omitted.*

### Point II: After Kathisma II
*   **Logic:** **Feast Priority.**
*   **Content:**
    1.  Sessional of the Feast.
    2.  Glory, Both now: Sessional of the Feast.

### Point III: After Polyeleos
*   **Logic:** **Feast Priority.**
*   **Content:**
    1.  Magnification (Velychannia) of the Feast.
    2.  Sessional of the Feast (Polyeleos Sessional).
    3.  Glory, Both now: Sessional of the Feast.
    *   *Note: Sometimes the Hypakoe of the Feast is taken here if specified.*

### Point IV: After Ode III
*   **Logic:** **The Shift (or Hypakoe Replacement).**
*   **Content:**
    *   *If the Feast has a Hypakoe:*
        1.  **Hypakoe** of the Feast.
    *   *If no Hypakoe:*
        1.  Sessional of the Feast.
    *   *The Kontakion/Ikos of the Feast usually stays at Ode VI unless superseded.*

### Point V: After Ode VI
*   **Logic:** **Feast Supremacy.**
*   **Content:**
    1.  Kontakion of the Feast.
    2.  Ikos of the Feast.
    *   *The Resurrectional Kontakion/Ikos is usually suppressed or moved.*

---

## Scenario 3: Weekday + Polyeleos Saint
**Context:** A Polyeleos Saint (Rank 3) on a Tuesday.

### Point I: After Kathisma
*   **Logic:** **Saint Priority.**
*   **Content:**
    1.  Sessional of the Saint.
    2.  Glory: Sessional of the Saint (or 2nd Sessional).
    3.  Both now: Theotokion.

### Point II: After Kathisma
*   **Logic:** **Saint Priority.**
*   **Content:**
    1.  Sessional of the Saint.
    2.  Glory: Sessional of the Saint.
    3.  Both now: Theotokion.

### Point III: After Polyeleos
*   **Logic:** **Saint Priority.**
*   **Content:**
    1.  Magnification.
    2.  Polyeleos Sessional of the Saint.
    3.  Glory, Both now: Theotokion.
    *   *No Hypakoe on Weekdays (except Bright Week).*

### Point IV: After Ode III
*   **Logic:** **Sessional Only.**
*   **Content:**
    1.  Sessional of the Saint.
    2.  Glory, Both now: Theotokion.
    *   *Kontakion stays at Ode VI.*

### Point V: After Ode VI
*   **Logic:** **Kontakion Slot.**
*   **Content:**
    1.  Kontakion of the Saint.
    2.  Ikos of the Saint.

---

## Scenario 4: Saint with "Shifted" Kontakion (The Indiction Model)
**Context:** Sept 1 (Indiction + St. Simeon) on Weekday.
*Specific stacking when multiple ranks coexist.*

### Point IV: After Ode III
*   **Logic:** **Complex Stack.**
*   **Content:**
    1.  Sessional of Indiction.
    2.  Sessional of Saint.
    3.  Glory: Sessional of Martyrs.
    4.  Both now: Theotokion.

### Point V: After Ode VI
*   **Logic:** **Main Feast Sequence.**
*   **Content:**
    1.  Kontakion of the Indiction.
    2.  Ikos of the Indiction.
    *   *The Saint's Kontakion is presumably recited at the Hours or suppressed, unless explicitly "Shifted" to Ode 3 in the rubrics.*

## The "Hypakoe" Rules

1.  **Availability:** Found in the Octoechos (Resurrectional) and Great Feasts (Menaion/Triodion).
2.  **Sunday Slot:** Always **Slot III** (After Polyeleos).
    *   *Quote:* "After Kathisma 17... immediately... Hypakoe of the current tone." (Dolnytsky Pt II).
3.  **Feast Slot:** Usually **Slot IV** (After Ode III) OR Slot III depending on specific Feast structure.
    *   *Pascha:* Hypakoe acts as Sessional after Ode III.
    *   *Nativity:* Hypakoe often appears after Ode III in some traditions, but Dolnytsky places specific Sessional hymns there.

## The "Kontakion Shift" Rules

1.  **The Rule of Displacement:** If the "High Place" (Ode VI) is occupied by a higher-ranking Kontakion (e.g., Resurrection on Sunday), the lower-ranking Kontakion (e.g., Saint) **must** yield.
2.  **Destination:** The yielded Kontakion moves to **Ode III** (Slot IV).
3.  **Combination:** It sits alongside the Sessional Hymn of Ode III.
    *   *Order:* Kontakion + Sessional (usually).
