Based on the **Dolnytsky Typikon** (Parts I, III, and IV), here is the fully unabridged structural outline for **Great Compline (Pavechirnya velyka)**.

This service is structurally distinct because it is divided into **three separate parts**, each with its own specific Logic Gates depending on whether the service is **Lenten** (Penitential) or **Festal** (Vigil).

### **Service Selector Logic**

* **Trigger Condition A (Lenten Standard):** Monday, Tuesday, Wednesday, Thursday of Great Lent.
* **Trigger Condition B (Festal Vigil):** Eves of Nativity, Theophany, and Annunciation.

---

### **PART I: The Dogmatic Section**

* **The Blessing:** "Blessed is our God...".
* **The Opening:** "Glory to Thee... Heavenly King... Trisagion... Psalms 4, 6, 12, 24, 30, 90."
* **The Hymn "God is With Us" (Isaiah 8:8-18):**
* **Resolve Case A (Lenten):** Sung in Tone 6 with the Lenten melody.
* **Resolve Case B (Vigil):** Sung with the Solemn (Festal) melody.


* **The Reader's Response:**
* "The day is passed, I thank Thee, O Lord...".


* **The Creed:** Recited.
* **The Prayer:** "O All-holy Lady, Theotokos..."
* **The Trisagion:** Standard sequence.
* **The Troparia of Part I:**
* **Resolve Case A (Lenten):** "The bodiless nature of the Cherubim..." (Sung with prostrations).
* **Resolve Case B (Vigil):**
* **Action:** Suppress "Bodiless Nature."
* **Insert:** **Troparion of the Feast** (Menaion).




* **The Conclusion of Part I:**
* Kyrie (40 times).
* "More honorable than the Cherubim..."
* **Prayer of St. Basil:** "O Lord, Lord, who deliverest us..."



---

### **PART II: The Penitential Section**

* **The Opening:** "O Come, Let us Worship..."
* **The Psalms:**
* Psalm 50 ("Have mercy on me...").
* Psalm 101 ("O Lord, hear my prayer...").


* **The Prayer of Manasseh:** "O Lord Almighty, God of our Fathers...".
* **The Trisagion:** Standard sequence.
* **The Troparia of Part II:**
* **Resolve Case A (Lenten):** "Have mercy on us, O Lord..." (Troparia of Repentance).
* **Resolve Case B (Vigil):**
* **Action:** Often suppressed or recited quickly without prostrations (Dolnytsky is less explicit on the suppression here compared to Part I, but standard Vigil practice minimizes the penitential character).




* **The Conclusion of Part II:**
* Kyrie (40 times).
* "More honorable..."
* **Prayer of St. Mardarius:** "O God and Master, Father Almighty..."



---

### **PART III: The Canon & Praises (The Primary Variable)**

* **The Opening:** "O Come, Let us Worship..."
* **The Psalms:**
* Psalm 69 ("O God, make haste...").
* Psalm 142 ("O Lord, hear my prayer...").


* **The Small Doxology:** Read (Recited).
* **The Canon Slot (Logic Gate from Part 4):**
* **Resolve Case A (Lenten Standard):**
* **Action:** Insert **Canon of the Saint of the Day** (moved from Matins).


* **Resolve Case B (Clean Week - Mon/Tue/Wed/Thu):**
* **Action:** Insert **Great Canon of St. Andrew of Crete** (Divided into 4 parts).


* **Resolve Case C (Vigil):**
* **Action:** Insert **Canon of the Feast** (Menaion).




* **The Axion Estin:** "It is truly proper..."
* **The Trisagion:** Standard sequence.
* **The "Lord of Hosts" Slot (Hymnody Variable):**
* **Resolve Case A (Lenten):**
* **Insert:** **"O Lord of Hosts, be with us"** (Tone 6) with verses from Psalm 150.


* **Resolve Case B (Vigil):**
* **Action:** Suppress "O Lord of Hosts."
* **Insert:** **Kontakion of the Feast** (Menaion).




* **The Conclusion of Part III:**
* Kyrie (40 times).
* **Prayer:** "O Lord, who at all times..."
* **Prayer:** "O Lord, God of our Fathers..." (If Lenten).



---

### **IV. The Dismissal Sequence**

* **The Prostrations (Logic Gate):**
* **Resolve Case A (Lenten):**
* **Action:** Insert **Prayer of St. Ephrem** (4 or 16 prostrations depending on the week).


* **Resolve Case B (Vigil):**
* **Action:** Omit St. Ephrem.




* **The Final Dismissal:**
* **Resolve Case A (Lenten):**
* **Prayer:** "O Immaculate, Undefiled..." (Prayer to the Theotokos).
* **Prayer:** "And give us, O Master..." (Prayer to Christ).
* **Rite of Forgiveness:** "Bless, holy fathers and brethren, and forgive me..." (Mutual prostrations).


* **Resolve Case B (Vigil):**
* **Transition:** If a Vigil, the service may transition directly into the **Litia** (Blessing of Bread/Wheat/Wine/Oil) and then into Matins.
