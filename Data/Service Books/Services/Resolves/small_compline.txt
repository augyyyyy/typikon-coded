To fulfill the "Study-Encyclopedia" requirement, the engine must treat **Small Compline (Pavechirnya mala)** not as a static text, but as a dynamic framework that synthesizes rules from all five parts of the **Dolnytsky Typikon**.

Below is the **Unabridged Structural Outline** with specific **Logic Gates** linking strictly to the Typikon's constituent parts.

---

### **I. The Enarxis (Opening)**

* **The Blessing:** "Blessed is our God..."
* **The Usual Beginning:**
* **Logic Gate (Part 4 - Triodion/Pentecostarion):**
* **Standard:** Recite "Glory to Thee... O Heavenly King... Trisagion... O Come Let Us Worship...".
* **Pascha to Ascension:** Omit "O Heavenly King."
* **Bright Week:** **STOP.** Redirect to `struct_compline_paschal` (Paschal Hours).




* **The Fixed Psalms:**
* **Psalm 50:** "Have mercy on me, O God".
* **Psalm 69:** "O God, make haste to help me".
* **Psalm 142:** "O Lord, hear my prayer".



---

### **II. The Doxology & Creed (Part 1 Base)**

* **The Small Doxology:**
* **Rubric:** Read (Recited), not sung.


* **The Nicene Creed:**
* **Rubric:** Read.



---

### **III. The Canon Slot (The Multi-Part Variable)**

This slot is the primary collision point for the Octoechos, Menaion, and Triodion.

* **Logic Gate A: The Default (Part 1 & 2):**
* **Condition:** Standard Weekday (Mon-Thu outside Lent) or Friday Evening (outside Lent).
* **Action:** Insert **Canon to the Theotokos** (Octoechos). Dolnytsky typically prescribes the *Supplicatory Canon* in the Tone of the Week.


* **Logic Gate B: The Feast (Part 3 - Menaion):**
* **Condition:** Rank 2 (Forefeast) or Rank 3 (Afterfeast).
* **Action:** Insert **Canon of the Feast** from the Menaion.
* **Precedence:** Menaion supersedes Octoechos.


* **Logic Gate C: The Lenten Friday (Part 4 - Triodion):**
* **Condition:** Friday Evening of Great Lent.
* **Action:** Insert **Canon of the Akathist to the Theotokos**.


* **Logic Gate D: Pre-Sanctified Night (Part 4 - Triodion):**
* **Condition:** Evenings of Clean Week or Holy Week (if Small Compline is used by exception).
* **Action:** Insert the **Three-Canted Canon (Theodion)** appointed for the day.



---

### **IV. The Axion & Trisagion**

* **Axion Estin:** "It is truly proper to glorify thee...".
* **The Trisagion Prayers:** "Holy God... All-Holy Trinity... Our Father...".

---

### **V. The Troparia Stack (The Temple & Rank Variable)**

This section requires the most complex logic, pulling specifically from **Part 5 (Temple Rubrics)**.

#### **Scenario 1: Saturday Evening (Sunday Cycle)**

* **Logic (Part 1/2):** Sunday supersedes all others.
* **Slot:** **Kontakion of the Resurrection** (Tone of the Week).

#### **Scenario 2: Standard Weekday (Mon/Tue/Thu)**

* **Slot 1 (Temple - Part 5):** Troparion of the **Temple** (Church Dedication).
* **Slot 2 (Day - Part 1):** Troparion of the **Day of the Week**.
* *Mon:* Angels / *Tue:* Baptist / *Thu:* Apostles & St. Nicholas.


* **Slot 3 (Saint - Part 3):** Troparion of the **Saint of the Day** (Menaion).
* **Glory:** Kontakion of the **Saint** (Menaion).
* *Exception:* If the Saint has no Kontakion, use Kontakion of the Temple.


* **Both Now:** Kontakion of the **Day** (Octoechos).
* *Tue:* "To thee, O Lord..." / *Thu:* "To thee, O Lord..."



#### **Scenario 3: Great Feast Period (Part 3)**

* **Condition:** Forefeast or Afterfeast.
* **Slot 1:** Troparion of the **Feast**.
* **Glory/Both Now:** Kontakion of the **Feast**.

#### **Scenario 4: Lenten Friday (Part 4)**

* **Condition:** Akathist served.
* **Slot 1:** **Kontakion of the Akathist** ("To thee, the Champion Leader").

---

### **VI. The Prayers & Dismissal**

* **The Kyrie:** "Lord, have mercy" (40 times).
* **Fixed Prayer 1:** "Thou Who at all times..." (Prayer of the Hours).
* **Fixed Prayer 2:** "O Spotless, Undefiled..." (Paul the Monk).
* **Fixed Prayer 3:** "And give us, O Master..." (Antiochus).
* **The Intercession:** "Most glorious Ever-Virgin...".
* **The Dismissal Proper:**
* **Logic Gate (Part 4 - Lent):**
* **Standard:** "Christ our true God..."
* **Lenten:** Extended dismissal invoking the Saint of the Day and the Patron of the Temple.




* **The Lenten Conclusion (Part 4):**
* **Condition:** If rubrics prescribe (e.g., certain Lenten Fridays or Monastic use).
* **Action:** Append **Prayer of St. Ephrem** (with prostrations) and the **Mutual Forgiveness** ("Bless, holy fathers...").


* **Final Litany:** "Let us pray for the Ecumenical Pontiff...".
