Sunday/Festal Matins

Based on the **Dolnytsky Typikon** (Parts 1â€“5), here is the fully unabridged structural outline for **Sunday & Festal Great Matins (Utrenya Velyka)**.

This is the most complex service in the Byzantine Rite. This outline serves **Sundays**, **Great Feasts**, and **Saints with Polyeleos/Vigil** (Rank 3+).

### **Service Selector Logic**

* **Trigger A:** Sunday Morning.
* **Trigger B:** Feast of Rank 3 (Polyeleos), Rank 4 (Vigil), or Rank 5 (Great Feast).
* **Trigger C:** Bright Week? **STOP.** Load `struct_matins_paschal`.

---

### **I. The Royal Office (The Enarxis)**

* **Logic Gate (Vigil Connection):**
* **Resolve Case A (Vigil Served):** If Matins follows Great Vespers/Litya immediately:
* **Action:** **SKIP** the Royal Office.
* **Start:** "Glory to God in the highest..." -> Hexapsalmos.


* **Resolve Case B (Separate Service):**
* **Action:** Begin with "Blessed is our God..."
* **Royal Psalms:** Psalms 19 & 20.
* **Troparia:** "O Lord, save Thy people..."
* **Censing:** The Priest censes the entire church during the reading.





---

### **II. The Six Psalms (Hexapsalmos)**

* **The Reading:** Psalms 3, 37, 62, 87, 102, 142.
* *Rubric:* Read with compunction; no movement in the church.


* **The Morning Prayers:** Read silently by the Priest in front of the Holy Doors.
* **The Great Litany:** "In peace let us pray to the Lord..."

---

### **III. "God is the Lord" & Troparia**

* **The Proclamations:**
* "God is the Lord and hath appeared unto us..." (Sung in the Tone of the Troparion).


* **The Troparia Stack (Part 2 & 5):**
* **Scenario A (Sunday + Saint Rank 5):**
* Troparion Resurrection (2x) -> Glory -> Saint -> Both Now -> Theotokion.


* **Scenario B (Great Feast):**
* Troparion of the Feast (3x).


* **Scenario C (Sunday + Saint Rank 3/4):**
* Troparion Resurrection (2x) -> Glory -> Saint -> Both Now -> Theotokion.





---

### **IV. The Psalter (Kathismata)**

* **Logic Gate (Day of Week):**
* **Resolve Case A (Sunday):**
* **Kathisma 1:** Read -> Sessional Hymns (Resurrectional).
* **Kathisma 2:** Read -> Sessional Hymns (Resurrectional).


* **Resolve Case B (Feast on Weekday):**
* **Kathisma:** Usually the Kathisma of the Day (e.g., Monday = Kathisma 4) or specific Festal antiphons if prescribed.





---

### **V. The Polyeleos (The High Point)**

* **The Polyeleos Psalms:**
* Psalms 134 ("Praise the Name of the Lord") and 135 ("O give thanks") are SUNG.


* **The Megalynarion (Velychannya) Logic:**
* **Resolve Case A (Sunday - Standard):** **OMITTED**. (Sunday focus is Evlogitaria).
* **Resolve Case B (Feast / Saint Rank 3+):** **INSERT** the Megalynarion of the Feast/Saint with selected Psalm verses.


* **The Evlogitaria (Resurrectional Hymns):**
* **Resolve Case A (Sunday):** **INSERT** "Blessed art Thou, O Lord... The assembly of angels...".
* **Resolve Case B (Feast on Weekday):** **OMITTED** (Unless Lazarus Saturday).


* **Small Litany & Hypakoe:**
* Hypakoe of the Tone (Sunday) or Feast.


* **Hymns of Ascent (Anavathmoi):**
* **Sunday:** Antiphons of the current Tone.
* **Feast (Weekday):** Usually Antiphon 1 of Tone 4 ("From my youth").



---

### **VI. The Gospel Cycle**

* **The Prokeimenon:**
* **Sunday:** Resurrectional Prokeimenon of the Tone.
* **Feast:** Festal Prokeimenon.


* **The Matins Gospel:**
* **Sunday:** One of the **11 Eothina Gospels** (cycling).
* **Feast:** Specific Gospel of the Feast.


* **The Hymn of Resurrection:**
* "In that we have beheld the Resurrection of Christ..."
* *Rubric:* Sung on Sundays; Read or Omitted on some Weekday Feasts (though often sung on Great Feasts).


* **Psalm 50:** Read.
* **The Post-Gospel Troparia (The "Glory" Slot):**
* **Resolve Case A (Sunday):**
* Glory: "Through the prayers of the **Apostles**..."
* Both Now: "Through the prayers of the **Theotokos**..."
* Sticheron: "Jesus, having risen from the tomb..."


* **Resolve Case B (Feast/Saint):**
* Glory: "Through the prayers of **[Saint's Name]**..."
* Both Now: "Through the prayers of the **Theotokos**..."
* Sticheron: Specific Sticheron of the Feast (Menaion).





---

### **VII. The Canon (The Hymnography)**

* **Structure:** Odes 1, 3, 4, 5, 6, 7, 8, 9. (Ode 2 is omitted outside Lent).
* **Content Logic (Part 2):**
* **Sunday:**
1. Canon of Resurrection (Octoechos).
2. Canon of the Cross-Resurrection (Octoechos).
3. Canon of the Theotokos (Octoechos).
4. Canon of the Saint (Menaion).


* **Feast:**
1. Canon of the Feast (First).
2. Canon of the Feast (Second) - if applicable.




* **The Katavasia (Logic Gate):**
* **Resolve:** Depends on the Calendar Period (e.g., "I shall open my mouth" vs. "Christ is Born").


* **Small Litanies:** After Odes 3 and 6.
* **Kontakion/Ikos:** After Ode 6.
* **The Magnificat (Ode 9):**
* **Resolve Case A (Sunday/Standard):** Sing "My soul magnifies the Lord" with "More Honorable."
* **Resolve Case B (Great Feast):** **OMIT** Magnificat. Sing the **Festal Megalynarion (Refrain)** and Ode 9 Irmos.



---

### **VIII. The Praises (Lauds) & Doxology**

* **The Holy is the Lord:** Sung in the Tone of the Week (Sunday) or Feast.
* **The Psalms of Praise:** Psalms 148, 149, 150.
* **The Stichera on the Praises:**
* **Count:** usually 8 (Sunday) or 4/6 (Feast).
* **Logic:** 4 Octoechos + 4 Menaion (if Saint Rank 3+).


* **The Doxastikon (Glory):**
* **Sunday:** **Gospel Sticheron** (Corresponding to the Eothinon Gospel read earlier).
* **Feast:** Glory of the Feast.


* **The Theotokion (Both Now):**
* **Sunday:** "Most Blessed art Thou, O Virgin..." (Fixed Text).
* **Feast:** Festal Theotokion.


* **The Great Doxology:**
* **Rubric:** **SUNG** (Solemn Melody). This is the defining mark of Festal Matins.


* **The Post-Doxology Troparion:**
* **Sunday (Odd Tones):** "Today salvation has come..."
* **Sunday (Even Tones):** "Having risen from the tomb..."
* **Feast:** Troparion of the Feast.



---

### **IX. The Dismissal**

* **Litanies:** "Have mercy on us..." and "Let us complete our morning prayer..."
* **The Dismissal Proper:**
* **Sunday:** "May **Christ our True God, risen from the dead**..."
* **Feast (Weekday):** "May **Christ our True God**..." (No Resurrectional phrase).
* **Mentions:** Saint of the Day, Patron of Temple, Saint of the Feast.


* **The First Hour:** Usually read immediately following Matins.
